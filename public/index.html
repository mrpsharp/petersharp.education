Educational Consultancy

INSET and teacher development

I am passionate about teacher improvement and have worked with schools of all types across the UK both delivering INSET and working with leaders to support teachers, working both in person and online. As an inspector with the Independent Schools Inspectorate I watch many lessons in a wide variety of schools every year and have a deep understanding of how to support successful pupil progress. I have delivered INSET in a range of schools, working with a many organisations including NACE, the Network for Learning, and Education Conferences UK.

University advice

I can help students understand the options available, manage their application and prepare for entry tests and interviews.

Physics tuition

I have particular expertise in preparing students for the most selective universities, including the Oxford PAT, and Cambridge NSAA and ENGAA.

My story

After studying an MA in Natural Sciences at the University of Cambridge and an MSc. at Imperial College, I worked for a charity aiming to support MPs and Peers in their engagement in scientific issues. Whilst working in parliament, I found my passion was explaining scientific ideas and I moved on to study a PGCSE in Secondary Science at the Institute of Education.

After a spell teaching Physics and Chemistry at Highgate School I moved to Westminster School as a Physics teacher. Over time I moved into senior management, ending up as Director of Teaching and Learning and then Deputy Head (Academic), overseeing the whole academic programme of one of the most successful schools in the United Kingdom. There I gained an insight in supporting students into the most competitive universities in the UK and overseas, as well as the potential of online learning during the COVID-19 pandemic.

In 2021 I left school leadership to focus on my twin passions of supporting schools to continuously improve and achieve the best outcomes for their students and supporting the most able and ambitious physics students to successfully take the next step in their education.

I am also passionate about philosophy, outdoor education and the environment. I have partially completed a degree in Philosophy at Birkbeck and continue to read widely in epistemology, metaphysics and the theory of time. 

I am also an examiner for Cambridge International, Chair of Governors at Christ's School, Richmond and live off-grid on the Kennet & Avon canal.
